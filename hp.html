Overview

During the summer of 2023 I joined HP Inc. for a 12 week internship with the Print Design department, specifically on the Setup & Onboarding team. On top of familiarizing myself with the HP print ecosystem, I streamlined efficiency by designing a system where expert users could quickly identify pain points based on their own insights as well as the insights of other experts.

Role: User Experience Design Intern

Scope: Product Strategy & Interaction Design

Tools: Miro, Figma, Excel, Power Automate, Forms, JIRA

The Problem

A heuristic checklist was established many years ago but never improved upon. The checklist is very difficult for expert users to fill out while also conducting an Unboxing and Setup experience of a new printer. The 500 questions needed copy updates and content revisions along with alignment with target user experiences. Additionally, expert users rarely if ever utilized it because of the length of the checklist and the fact that there was no way to efficiently process the data.

Format of Heuristic Checklist & Example Heuristic Question

Therefore, I created the following problem statement to address the issues I was trying to solve by redesigning the heuristic evaluation checklist...

How might I find a way to standardize the checklist and make it quicker to fill out and automate the process of receiving data quickly so that users can actually utilize the data to determine pain points more efficiently?

The Solution - high level overivew

After conducting more user research, I was able to create a system that addressed the pain points from my target audience: expert printer evaluators.

  1. The system created has several different steps as referenced by the chart above:
    1. The expert printer evaluators (users) would first answer rating scale questions (1-5) on one of four Forms (each form corresponding to a different part of the setup process).
    2. If the question was not applicable, there was an option to indicate so as well.
  2. Utilizing Power Automate, the data from one expert user’s unique response would be populated onto an Excel Spreadsheet.
  3. Using Excel’s formulas, the process was further automated so that the spreadsheet would produce a summary of what heuristics were met and to what degree.
  4. This new way of heuristically evaluating a printer allowed experts to analyze the results of their unboxing and setup process more quantitatively.

The Process - more about how I got from the problem to the solution

01 Understanding My Users

My Manager

My manager communicated the initial problem to me of the heuristic evaluation checklist being too difficult to use efficiently and I decided to explore the problem more.

5 Co-Workers

Based on the recommendation from my manager, I interviewed 5 of my co-workers who had performed heuristic evaluations using the old checklist to determine their specific pain points.

Myself

After performing a few evaluations of printers using the evaluation checklist, I was able to determine a few more specific pain points helped me further define the problem.

Insights Gathered

  1. The length of the checklist is too long to feasibly fill out while conducting a heuristic evaluation of a printer simultaneously.
  2. Due to the size of the checklist, it is too tedious to navigate and requires the user to input at least 10 clicks to answer a heuristic question.
  3. After inputting all their data, a user is not able to see a data summary immediately, but rather needs to navigate through the entire checklist again.
  4. Many of the questions were outdated as printer unboxing steps had been updated but the checklist had not been updated alongside this.
  5. The format of the questions was very repetitive, requiring users to enter a response in both the formats of yes/no questions and a rating scale question.

02 Ideation

Redefining the Scope

I started to redefine the scope of the existing checklist by trying to determine which format of questions to include in the improved version of the checklist. I wanted to determine if Yes/No/NA questions or Rating Scale Questions (1-5) would be more informational. Based on feedback from users during preliminary user interviews, I realized many users were found answering both questions to be redundant.


Making Improvements

To understand which questions to include and how to appropriately word them, I reached out to co-workers who were experts in the respective parts of the process: packaging, setup manual, physical setup, and online setup. Working with these experts, I was able to determine which questions to include and which questions were no longer applicable to the setup process. I also worked on the copy of the questions to ensure that they were easy to decipher.


Figuring Out The Format

I figured I should distribute the heuristic checklist on a Microsoft Form as this was the system most of my co-workers were already familiar with. Additionally, I used Power Automate behind the scenes to connect this to an Excel Sheet and automatically produce a data summary of responses.

03 User Testing

Working Out The Details

To determine which question formation was better liked by users, I presented my 5 co-workers with two different forms and employed the A/B testing method to determine what was received better. I asked expert users to heuristically evaluate printers in conjunction with using the survey.

All 5 users preferred B due to the ability to enter a more variable score.

04 The Final Solution

How It All Turned Out

This is the format our final solution took. It included a qualitative data report can be easily inserted into reports and read by higher ups.

Additional Changes

I incorporated 3 different forms for each section to

  1. cut down length of form (50 questions per section)
  2. and because during user interviews, people mentioned they only performed a part of the total evaluation occasionally.

Making Sure the Form Outlasted Me

  1. I created a guide detailing the process of how to operate and adjust the form from the backend should any changes ever need to be made.
  2. This is to ensure the form outlasts my time at HP and expert users can continue to benefit and improve from it and in turn help our target users.

My Impact

Streamlined efficiency by creating a system which motivates expert users to utilize it through...

  • an uncomplicated method of data collection
  • a more through & automated

Standardized the evaluation process to create the following benefits

  • Set & Standard criteria prevents biases - keeps experts from paying too much attention to one aspect of the Setup & Onboarding experience or neglecting any aspects thus skewing the collected data
  • Even novices will be able to easily understand what criteria to consider during evaluations which streamlines the onboarding process
